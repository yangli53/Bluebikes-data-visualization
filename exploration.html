Boston Bluebikes Data Exploration and Visualization¶

by Yang Li¶

Preliminary Wrangling¶

Bluebikes is Metro Boston's public bike share program, with over 2,500+ bikes and more than 260+ stations across Boston, Brookline, Cambridge and Somerville. I downloaded its Q2 data of 2019 (April, May and June) to explore bike use in this area. I did some preliminary wrangling:

  1. Dropped unnecessary columns start station id, start station latitude, start station longitude, end station id, end station latitude, end station longitude and bikeid.
  2. Replace space in column names with underline.
  3. Changed data type of starttime and stoptime to datetime
  4. Replace gender column values: 0=Female, 1=Male, 2=Other
  5. Changed data type of usertype and gender to category

What is the structure of your dataset?¶

There are 663,800 individual rides in this dataset with 8 features:

  • tripduration in sec
  • starttime
  • stoptime
  • start station name
  • end station name
  • usertype
  • birth year
  • gender

What is/are the main feature(s) of interest in your dataset?¶

I want to profile Boston Bluebikes users' biking habits by analyzing their demographics and bike usage. This can help to decide if the current pricing strategy should be improved and find potential business opportunities.

What features in the dataset do you think will help support your investigation into your feature(s) of interest?¶

There are two types of user: subscribers are annual or monthly members; customers are single trip or day pass users.

To start with, I want to get users' demographics, including birth year distribution and gender distribution.

Second, I want to identify the differences of biking habits between subscribers and customers. I will analyze their bike usage, including count of rides and ride duration, by hour and by weekday.

Finally, I want to find the top 10 start stations, end stations and routes by each type of user.

# create a function to create a DataFrame for heatmap
def heatmap_df(data, cat1, cat2, num):
    df = data.groupby([cat1, cat2]).mean()[num]
    df = df.reset_index(name='count')
    df = df.pivot(index=cat2, columns=cat1, values='count')
    return df
